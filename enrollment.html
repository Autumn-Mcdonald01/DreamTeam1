Enrollment Information

Eligibility

Beginning with the 2020-2021 school year, students will be eligible to
join National Honor Society as sophomores or juniors. Students must have
a minimum 3.25 GPA and have completed the required number of community
service hours (20 for sophomores, 40 for juniors). All eligible students
will receive an invitation to apply for membership.

Selection

Students must complete an application that includes information about
their community service and leadership activities as well academic
achievements. Students also write a brief response explaining how they
uphold the NHS pillars. Candidate applications are reviewed by a member
faculty council. The chapter sponsor and co-sponsors have no part in the
selecton of new members. Faculty council members score applications
using a rubric. Students that meet all criteria will be issued a
membership invitation.

Induction

New members are inducted in a ceremony. Families, teachers, and
community members are invited to attend. New members will receive a NHS
membership pin.

Membership Obligations

All duly inducted members must abide by the following constitutional obligations:

Dues

Students selected for membership are asked to pay annual dues in the
amount of $20. We are a small, growing chapter, and dues help cover the
cost of induction ceremonies, the annual chapter fee paid to National
Honor Society, and other activities as needed. If a student is unable to
meet this obligation, the chapter adviser should be notified as soon as
possible.

Maintain Chapter Standards

Once inducted, students must continue to maintain the standards of the
chapter. Students must maintain or exceed a 3.25 GPA. Academic
eligibility will be reviewed at the end of each semester. Students must
also continue to meet or exceed the community service requirements.
Students must continue to demonstrate exemplary leadership and character
in both school and the community.

Meetings

Students are required to attend all chapter meetings. NHS meets on the
first Friday of each month and at other times as determined to be
necessary. Students will be given adequate notice of additional meetings
times and should make every effort to attend. If attendance is not
possible, the student must notify the chapter adviser as soon as
possible. Students are responsible for obtaining any information missed.

Service Projects

The chapter will sponsor at least one service project a year. Members
are expected to actively participate in the planning and execution of
the project. Members are also expected to perform additional service
according to their own talents and interests. Records of service
activities should be submitted to the chapter adviser in a timely
manner.
